Farmville – History and Evolution

In June 2009 Zynga released Farmville for free on Facebook. The launch of Farmville was very successful as just after four days after launch it was breaking records with one million active users daily. Within six months Farmville had thirty million active users. At first Zynga was against the idea of creating Farmville as generally they performed poorly but CEO Mark Pincus believed that with Facebook’s rise and social capabilities that Farmville can thrive. Zynga quickly became a money making machine estimated to be worth more than 4 Billion dollars in 2010.

To keep Farmville fresh and new Zynga constantly released several monthly updates. These updates consisted of features and adjustments that the community requested, a variety of new features that expanded the farm as well as bug fixes and general maintenance of the game. Some of the updates that Zynga released were an array of crops, both limited and permanent versions. They added themes for farm expansions such as English Countryside, Winter Wonderland, and Hawaiian Paradise etc. They also added quests that need to be completed within a certain time period that offer limited edition items. As well as this Zynga kept the market items updated with new decorations, clothes, and buildings.
